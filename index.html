      About OnePark

      One park. One connected system.
      Nature, energy, and people working together.

      OnePark began as a question: what if a park could do more than sit quietly in a city — what if it could power homes, clean water, restore habitat, and still feel like the calmest place you visit all week? Six years and 500 acres later, that question became an operating system for the land.

      Every path, structure, and planting bed here belongs to a single loop. Rain becomes irrigation. Sunlight becomes electricity. Fallen leaves become soil. Visitors become stewards. Nothing is decorative — the beauty is a byproduct of the function.

      Ecological

      112 restored acres of native forest, wetland, and prairie, home to over 200 documented species.

      Technological

      A live sensor mesh of 4,200 nodes reads soil, air, water, and canopy health every 90 seconds.

      Human

      5 million annual visitors, 60 community programs, and a research campus open to every school in the region.

        Timeline

        From reclaimed rail yard
        to living system.

        2018

        A derelict rail yard

        140 acres of disused freight land is acquired by the city and set aside for public green space rather than development.

        2020

        Groundbreaking

        Soil remediation begins alongside the first native plantings; the solar canopy foundations go in before a single path is paved.

        2022

        First zones open

        The Botanical Gardens and Nature Trails welcome their first visitors while construction continues around them.

        2024

        Net zero achieved

        OnePark generates more energy than it consumes for the first time, six months ahead of schedule.

        Today

        500 acres, fully connected

        Twelve zones, a research campus, and a sensor mesh spanning the entire park operate as one continuously learning system.

        2028

        The next 300 acres

        Expansion into the adjoining floodplain will double wetland habitat and add a second research campus focused on climate-resilient agriculture.

        Innovation

        The quiet machinery
        behind the calm.

        None of it is visible from the trail. All of it is why the trail stays healthy.

        A living digital twin

        Every tree, sensor, and structure in OnePark exists as a real-time model. Planners test irrigation changes, storm scenarios, and canopy growth in simulation before touching a single acre of real ground.

        4,200 environmental sensors

        Soil moisture, canopy temperature, air quality, and water table depth are read every 90 seconds across the full 500 acres, feeding irrigation and energy systems automatically.

        Autonomous canopy drones

        A fleet of six drones surveys canopy health and wildlife activity weekly, flagging stress or disease long before it's visible from the ground.

        Storage that outlasts the sun

        Ninety megawatt-hours of on-site storage bank surplus solar and wind output, keeping the park's operations powered through every overcast week of the year.

        Global impact

        One blueprint.
        Nine cities building it.

        OnePark's water, energy, and sensor systems were published as an open reference design in 2024. Nine city governments are now adapting it for their own reclaimed land.
